Skies set the mood and connotation of many of your 3D scenes.

And many times they will be the main light source and background of your render.

This means that this light source will define most of the aspect of your project.


Choosing a good quality HDRI is an important step to the determine the final render output.

Accurate Lighting

Unclipped EVs

Our HDRIs have been retouched to display a correct sun intensity. This will ensure that the amount of light and shadows are correct.

This means that there will be sharp shadows when they should, and you will not have to worry about correcting or adding auxiliary light emitters.

Calibrated according to real world photographic values

Each HDRI was adjusted to match the same lighting condition than reality.

In simple words, night HDRIs will look darker than sunny days HDRIs at the same exposure.

This ensures a realistic lighting condition, and you will need to adjust the shutter speed of the camera (or EVs) just as you would need to do in the real world.

White balance

We have tuned them to ensure that the white balance is correctly set.

Consistency

The HDRIs will just work fine out of the box.

We have ensured that every HDRI in this pack has a consistent quality not only in terms of lighting, but also in the following features.

Carefully edited

We edited them to ensure that there are the least amount of artifacts as possible.

  • No sun flares.
  • No dust particles.
  • No birds (or UFOs?) .
  • No chromatic aberration.
  • No bad stitching.

Centered sun

For ease of use, you will find that each of them has the sun centered. This makes finding the right HDRI a much easier task when iterating.

14k and 2k versions

While the high resolution ones (14k px) are great for final renders (as they have lots of detail), the low resolution ones will let you iterate more easily when choosing the right HDRI for your scene because they are much faster to load.

Optimized

You will not only get very high-quality images, but also you will find that all of our panoramas are very optimized compared to many others.

This is mainly achieved by the HDR file format and the need to only show the light information from where it is important: the upper half of the dome.

The average size of our 14K ones is 80MB. To give you a reference most of the HDRIs at that same resolution can take up to 200MB in HDR or 1GB in EXR format.

Well organized and tagged

Each HDRI is semantically named to easily find the one you need.

Our naming convention sorts the HDRI according to their main relevant aspects.

These are: Resolution (High / Low) - Approx. % of clouds - Day time - Type of shadow + Unique code.

Preview image samples

We also provide sample preview images that will help you easily define which HDRI of our pack fits your project best.

That is a lot of time saved in just choosing the right HDRI!

Are you a Blender user?

If you are, then good news for you! This pack also contains a Blender Assets version of the HDRIs.

Below are some of the main advantages you will have.

Blender Assets ready

You will just need to drag and drop them, and you will have very handy controllers to tweak the HDRIs most "commonly adjusted" properties.

Categorized

The assets are categorized according to the same criteria that the file name has.

Rotate

Configure the angle of the HDRIs in Z axis to decide the illumination with precision, allowing for a wide variety of moods with the same sky.

Horizon line

Control where the horizon line falls with this offset slider.

Punch

An effect we added to our HDRIs system, that enhances their contrast and colours when enabled.

EVs

Although every HDRI has its EVs correctly calibrated, you can change the HDRIs EVs value with this slider.

Color Temperature

Tweak the aspect of the HDRI to be rather warmer or colder with this parameter, offseting it from its neutral white balance.

Gamma

If you are an advanced user, you can retouch the gamma to enhance or decrease the sun intensity influence.

Greyscale

Artistically retouch the sky with the greyscale slider. Combinable with the punch effect.

Eevee compatible

Since Blender 4.2, Eevee now supports HDRI lighting, so you will take advantage of this when using this pack!
