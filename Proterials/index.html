Key concepts

1 Proterial = Lots of possibilities

A key concept was to maximize the power of proceduralism.

This means that each Proterial is actually the spawn point of many output possibilities.

While many material packs offer numerous options, they often rely on simple tweaks like color changes. Instead, we focus on providing powerful starting points that you can easily customize to suit your needs.

For instance, we include 3 Car Paint materials + a set of color palettes & a color randomizer node. These will serve as a solid beginning that will look realistic from the moment of the load, and they have many sliders for you to play with and customize. All of this in combination will let you get lots of different outputs, from just 3 initial presets.

Another example is our Tiles material, which lets you change the mortar color, separation, roughness, pattern properties, and all you need, served in the easiest way possible, to get the variant that you want.

Now, if ‚Äú1 proterial = Lots of possibilities‚Äù, can you solve the ‚ÄúMany proterials = X‚Äù equation? ;)

High detail & high efficiency

You ask: How detailed is it? We say: Yes.

Jokes aside, we made them with a very high level of detail and optimized them as much as possible.

To give an example of our optimization approach, we decided not to rely on displacement to make our materials look good, as some procedural materials do. This is a fantastic option but it is extremely heavy to compute in an everyday production environment.

We decided to create our materials using the bump effect only by default. This way, the material ends up being much lighter.

Simple, systematic, and compatible

To create our materials collection we decided to systemize it as much as possible.

We decided to build a robust but simple system that can let you (and us) create many great-looking materials extremely fast and consistently.

Our "Building Blocks" are specialized group nodes designed to work like Lego pieces, enabling flexible and intuitive material creation.

This system seamlessly integrates with Blender‚Äôs ‚ÄúVanilla‚Äù workflow, requiring no new methods or learning curves. It‚Äôs a toolset that enhances your existing setup without disrupting it.

Real Principled

This shader node is meant to be an enhanced version of the already powerful Principled BSDF.

We have added a set of parameters to it that will let you create a realistic material in a matter of seconds.

This material node adds natural imperfections and characteristics that every material in real life tends to present.

Moreover, it is tuned to include advanced Blender ‚Äúhacks‚Äù to get some special effects like Quick Caustics, Dispersion, and Volume parameters, which are very useful when creating different transparent materials.

Tri-planar mapping and Real-world Scale by default

Sorry unwrapping, but you are very‚Ä¶ very hated‚Ä¶ So yes, by default our materials use the object coordinates and a very efficient tri-planar node to let you skip the UV mapping process.

Not only the textures have the option to be projected using a tri-planar method, but they will also have a real-world scale by default. This means that, if you scale the object, the texture will remain the same size.

And, if you need to, you can switch the texture coordinates to your liking.

Do the materials work on Eevee?

To be direct, we prefer to say ‚Äúno‚Äù to prevent creating expectations.

The materials are created to be used mainly on Cycles.

They can be tried to be loaded on Eevee but, sincerely, using heavy procedural materials on Eevee is not recommended yet. Mainly because of the shader compilation time and the responsiveness you get after loaded.

Hopefully, the moment Eevee makes an upgrade on its core, they will work as fast as in Cycles.

You still can bake the textures!

If you do so, Eevee will have no problem on showing them because they are much lighter. However, you lose the proceduralism.

If you are interested in this method, we recommend you to check some PBR baker add-ons on the market as they will help you a lot with this.
