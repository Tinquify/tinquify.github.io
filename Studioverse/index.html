Powerful Shot manager

Do you think it was over? Here is the cherry on top.

Render Manager

Our render manager complements perfectly your already native workflow with Blender. Create shots that store your current setup; view layer, camera (with its current aspect ratio, shutter speed, aperture and animations!), and world.

Create custom shots

Profesional renders are created with custom setups per shot. Not only that. You may want to test different lightings and setups during iterations or final renders.

Our addon lets you do these setups with ease, taking advantage of the great tools that Blender already has. Naturally, create shots with particular light conditions. Then save it as a shot. If you want to create another one, just duplicate it and do the necessary changes.

Have granular control

Change the resolution of the render per shot, adjust the camera properties like the aperture, ISO, shutter speed, show or hide properties that are relevant to you, filter the shots, re-order them, add a tag to one or all of them, etc… Tons of details are covered!

Physical Camera values

Play with real camera values to emulate the visual look that it would have in your Blender Viewport. And if you are unsure of what to set, use our custom presets to get started.

Render Queue and preview

Whether you had stills or animations, hit the Render queue button to start converting all of those shots into reality!

Viewlayers mixer and shot combinator

Do you need to create combinations? Easily create all possible combinations with our shot combinator and View layer utilities. Mix Cameras with view layers and worlds to create a multiverse of shots in just a few clicks.

What is the difference between the price tags?

We want to be really sincere with you.

We are a small studio and we know that not everybody has Pixar’s budget.

That is why we believe the fairest way to offer this product is by splitting it into usage ranges.

  • Indie
    • For individuals and small creators.
    • Up to USD 25k/year in combined gross income.
    • Up to 15 yearly add-on installations (≈1–3 seats guideline).
    • This tier may also be divided into subcategories, each offering different combinations of assets in terms of quantity and/or quality.
  • Pro
    • For professional creators and growing studios.
    • $25k–$250k/year gross income.
    • Up to 25 yearly add-on installations (≈10 seats guideline).
    • Up to 10 seats for non-software assets.
  • Company
    • For established businesses.
    • $250k–$1M/year gross income.
    • Up to 50 yearly add-on installations (≈20 seats guideline).
    • Up to 20 seats for non-software assets.
  • Enterprise
    • For large organizations.
    • $1M+/year gross income.
    • Up to 125 yearly add-on installations (≈50 seats guideline).
    • Up to 50 seats for non-software assets.
    • Additional licenses may be required for multi-division deployments.

To be clear:

  • All tiers include the same product and the same license. There are no differences in quality or functionality.
  • Revenue, seats, and installations are guidelines, not hard technical locks. They exist to keep things fair across different scales of use.
  • After purchase, you receive a commercial, royalty-free license, with:
    • no per-use or per-sale royalties
    • no time-based usage limits
    • no media reproduction limits
    • access to free updates and previous releases
    • yearly installation limits (tier-based) only for add-on products

Some components are licensed under the GNU GPL, while all other assets are covered by our commercial license.

We hope you understand that, we set our prices at what we believe is the lowest reasonable level for each tier, aiming to be both accessible and fair to the work behind the product.

To sum up, choosing a tier is based on honest self-assessment, and we decided to trust you.

Important notes

  • It is not 100% Eeveeproof. In other words, not everything works in Eevee.
    • We can say that 90% of the addon tools work with Eevee. Just some things don’t work because Blender itself does not support them yet. An example is the procedural lights (Areas and spotlights that uses nodes). However you will benefit of many tools anyways, and the lighting can be baked with our HDRI too (so you can use it in Eevee).
  • Proterials is the pack of materials we used for our renders. It is a design oriented high-quality and customizable set of shaders that complements neatly with Studioverse.
